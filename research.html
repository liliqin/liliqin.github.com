  Research Projects  

In the Ph.D. program, Lili Qin participated in two research projects. In an immunological project, she prepared the polyclonal and monoclonal antibodies against human cardiac troponin-I (cTn-I) by herself and assisted in developing the enzyme-linked immunosorbent assay which could be used in routine clinical test of myocardial infarction (MI). Her dissertation focused on the molecular mechanism through which Aurora-A, an oncogenic protein, promotes tumorigenesis. In this project, they screened the downstream targets of Aurora-A, and then concentrated on how Aurora-A regulates Cyclin B1. Some of the results have been published in Cancer Letters.

Please find a brief description of that work here.

The molecular mechanism of Aurora-A in tumorigenesis, 2006.3-2008.9
State Key Laboratory of Molecular Oncology (SKLMO), Cancer Institute & Hospital (CICAMS), Chinese Academy of Medical Sciences and Peking Union Medical College, Beijing China

Summary: The mitotic regulator Aurora-A is an oncogenic protein that is over-expressed in many types of human tumors. This research focuses on the underlying mechanism through which Aurora-A promotes tumorigenesis. In Aim 1, we will screen the downstream targets of Aurora-A. We found that Aurora-A induces expression changes of many proteins implicated in signaling pathways of cell cycle control and apoptosis. In Aim 2, we will concentrate on the molecular mechanism of Aurora-A in cell cycle control and in tumorigenesis. We found that overexpression of Aurora-A causes an elevation of Cyclin B1 expression. Cyclin B1 degradation is delayed in Aurora-A over-expressing cells, which depends on Aurora-A kinase activity. In contrast, Aurora-A RNAi enhances Cyclin B1 degradation. Furthermore, we found that Aurora-A overexpression reduces the interaction of Cyclin B1 with APC subunits. In human esophageal squamous cell carcinomas (ESCC), overexpression of Aurora-A was correlated with deregulated expression of Cyclin B1. Taken together, these findings suggest that overexpression of Aurora-A may stabilize Cyclin B1 through inhibiting its Ubiquitin-mediated degradation. Meanwhile, we found that Aurora-A interacts with Cyclin B1. Then the interaction domains and the role of Aurora-A kinase activity in the interaction were investigated. These results may provide new insight into the mechanism of how deregulated Aurora-A contributes to genomic instability and carcinogenesis.

The Enzyme-Linked-Immunosorbent Assay for cardiac troponin-I, 2004.9-2005.7
Institute of Radiation Medicine, Chinese Academy of Medical Sciences and Peking Union Medical College (PUMC), Tianjin, China

Summary: To improve the sensitivity and specificity of biochemical markers of myocardial infarction (MI), monoclonal antibodies against human cardiac troponin-I (cTn-I) are widely used to measure cTn-I in serum. In this project, we prepared polyclonal and monoclonal antibodies and developed the enzyme-linked immunosorbent assay for cTn-I measurement using monoclonal antibody. The results proved to be appropriate for routine clinical test and scientific research with broad application prospects.
