Aurora-A/CyclinB1 & Tumorigenesis

Mar 2006 - Sep 2008
State Key Laboratory of Molecular Oncology (SKLMO), Cancer Institute & Hospital (CICAMS)
Chinese Academy of Medical Sciences & Peking Union Medical College (CAMS&PUMC), Beijing China

The mitotic regulator Aurora-A is an oncogenic protein that is over-expressed in many types of human tumors. This research focuses on the underlying mechanism through which Aurora-A promotes tumorigenesis. In Aim 1, we will screen the downstream targets of Aurora-A. We found that Aurora-A induces expression changes of many proteins implicated in signaling pathways of cell cycle control and apoptosis. In Aim 2, we will concentrate on the molecular mechanism of Aurora-A in cell cycle control and in tumorigenesis. We found that overexpression of Aurora-A causes an elevation of Cyclin B1 expression. Cyclin B1 degradation is delayed in Aurora-A over-expressing cells, which depends on Aurora-A kinase activity. In contrast, Aurora-A RNAi enhances Cyclin B1 degradation. Furthermore, we found that Aurora-A overexpression reduces the interaction of Cyclin B1 with APC subunits. In human esophageal squamous cell carcinomas (ESCC), overexpression of Aurora-A was correlated with deregulated expression of Cyclin B1. Taken together, these findings suggest that overexpression of Aurora-A may stabilize Cyclin B1 through inhibiting its Ubiquitin-mediated degradation. Meanwhile, we found that Aurora-A interacts with Cyclin B1. Then the interaction domains and the role of Aurora-A kinase activity in the interaction were investigated. These results may provide new insight into the mechanism of how deregulated Aurora-A contributes to genomic instability and carcinogenesis.

Methods:

  • Cell culture and treatment
  • Protein purification and analysis (SDS-PAGE; Western Blotting)
  • Immunological techniques (IF; IHC)
  • Protein-protein interaction (IP; Pull-down assay)
  • Preparation and analysis of RNA (PCR; RT-PCR; agarose gel electrophoresis)
  • Recombinant DNA techniques (Preparation of plasmid DNA; Mutagenesis of cloned DNA; Enzymatic manipulation of DNA; cDNA cloning; Recombinant protein expression in E. coli and eukaryotic cells; Transformation; Transfection)
  • Computing tools (Primier5; SPSS; SigmaPlot; QuantityOne; EndNote)

Monoclonal Antibody of CTn-I

Sep 2004 - Jul 2005
Institute of Radiation Medicine, Tianjin China
Chinese Academy of Medical Sciences & Peking Union Medical College (CAMS&PUMC)

To improve the sensitivity and specificity of biochemical markers of myocardial infarction (MI), monoclonal antibodies against human cardiac troponin-I (cTn-I) are widely used to measure cTn-I in serum. In this project, we prepared polyclonal and monoclonal antibodies and developed the kit based on enzyme-linked immunosorbent assay for cTn-I measurement using monoclonal antibody. The results proved to be appropriate for routine clinical test and scientific research with broad application prospects.

Methods:

  • Cell culture
  • Polypeptide synthesis
  • Animal manipulation; Antiserum generation and monoclonal antibody isolation
  • Hybridoma technology
  • Protein immunological assay (ELISA)
  • Protein purification and analysis (SDS-PAGE)
