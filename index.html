Biography

Lili Qin obtained her Ph.D. in Radiation Medicine (Molecular Cell Biology) from Chinese Academy of Medical Sciences and Peking Union Medical College (PUMC) in January 2009. Then, she got an award from the NIH Visiting Program and started her research at NIH/NCI as a postdoctoral Visiting Fellow. She had got a Medicine Degree in July 1998, and completed the clinical training in the following 3 years.

During graduate study, Lili Qin got a perfect good record in major courses. In the first stage of her Ph.D. program, she participated in an immunological project at Institute of Radiation Medicine, PUMC. she prepared the polyclonal and monoclonal antibodies against human cardiac troponin-I (cTn-I) by herself and assisted in developing the enzyme-linked immunosorbent assay which could be used in routine clinical test of myocardial infarction (MI). From 2006, she joined the State Key Laboratory of Molecular Oncology (SKLMO), Cancer Institute & Hospital (CICAMS), PUMC to do her Ph.D. dissertation research. Her study there focused on the molecular mechanism through which Aurora-A, an oncogenic protein, promotes tumorigenesis. They screened the downstream targets of Aurora-A, and then concentrated on how Aurora-A regulates Cyclin B1. The results suggest that Aurora-A may stabilize Cyclin B1 through inhibiting its ubiquitin-mediated degradation. Furthermore, they found that Aurora-A interacts with Cyclin B1, and then the interaction domains and the roles of Aurora-A kinase activity in the interaction were also investigated. These findings may provide new insight into the mechanism of how deregulated Aurora-A contributes to genomic instability and carcinogenesis.

At NIH/NCI/LBMB, Lili Qin will do an extensive research on nautilus gene, a myogenic regulatory factors in Drosophila. She is interested in the functions of nautilus and the related underlying molecular mechanism. Also, she is interested in the regulation mechanism of nautilus expression.
