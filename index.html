  Welcome  

Lili Qin received her Ph.D. in Radiation Medicine (Cell Biology) from Chinese Academy of Medical Sciences and Peking Union Medical College in July 2008. She will start postdoctoral training on May 11 2009 at NIH/NCI. She got a Medicine Degree in China in July 1998, and completed the clinical training in the following 3 years.

In the Ph.D. program, she got a very good record in major courses and participated in two research projects. In an immunological project, she prepared the polyclonal and monoclonal antibodies against human cardiac troponin-I (cTn-I) by herself and assisted in developing the enzyme-linked immunosorbent assay which could be used in routine clinical test of myocardial infarction (MI). Her dissertation focused on the molecular mechanism through which Aurora-A, an oncogenic protein, promotes tumorigenesis. In this project, they screened the downstream targets of Aurora-A, and then concentrated on how Aurora-A regulates Cyclin B1. The results suggest that Aurora-A may stabilize Cyclin B1 through inhibiting its ubiquitin-mediated degradation. Furthermore, they found that Aurora-A interacts with Cyclin B1, and then the interaction domains and the roles of Aurora-A kinase activity in the interaction were also investigated. These findings may provide new insight into the mechanism of how deregulated Aurora-A contributes to genomic instability and carcinogenesis. Some of the results have been published in Cancer Letters.
